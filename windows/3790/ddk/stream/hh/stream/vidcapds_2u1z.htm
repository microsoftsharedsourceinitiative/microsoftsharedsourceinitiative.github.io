DirectShow Filters Used with the Stream Class Video Capture Drivers

DirectShow (formerly called Microsoft® ActiveMovie®) is the common client for video capture minidrivers. User-mode DirectShow filters are responsible for exposing the technology contained in the video capture minidriver to user mode applications.

There are four system-provided DirectShow filters that work with stream class driver to expose the underlying video capture minidriver functionality:

Filter (Functionality) Purpose DLL DirectShow Interfaces exposed
Video capture Provides output streams of digital video data and ancillary data streams. KSProxy.ax IAMAnalogVideoDecoder

IAMCameraControl

IAMVideoProcAmp

IAMDroppedFrames

IAMStreamConfig

IAMVideoControl

IAMVideoCompression

IAMBufferNegotiation
TV tuning Provides tuning control of analog TV, digital TV, FM and AM tuners. KSTVTune.ax IAMTVTuner
TV audio Provides control of TV audio such as SAP selection. KSXBar.ax IAMTVAudio
Crossbar Provides routing of video and audio streams. KSXBar.ax IAMCrossbar

Each of these functions (Video Capture, TV/radio tuning, TV audio and Crossbar) appears in a DirectShow filter graph as a separate filter exposing unique interfaces.

For more information about the DirectShow interfaces listed above, refer to the DirectShow SDK. The DirectShow SDK documentation also includes a sample application (AMCAP) that demonstrates how to construct the full range of both WDM and VfW capture graphs.
