COM Port Interrupt Sharing

Legacy COM ports on the same multiport board share a single interrupt. COM ports on a multiport board are identified by an index or a mask that is used to associate a port with its corresponding device object.

Standalone COM ports can share interrupts. However, a shared interrupt can only be used by one port at a time. When a port is opened, Serial allocates the port's interrupt to the port. When a port is closed, the driver releases the port's interrupt for use by another port.
