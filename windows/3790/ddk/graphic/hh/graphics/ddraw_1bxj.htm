DirectDraw Interfaces for Kernel-Mode Video Transport Support

The kernel-mode video transport must keep track of surface information for each surface it uses, and for each VPE object. This information must be updated every time DdUpdateOverlay or DdVideoPortUpdate is called for the surface or hardware video port. Before DirectDraw sends this information to the kernel mode video transport, it calls one of two driver functions: DdSyncSurfaceData or DdSyncVideoPortData. These functions allow the driver to fill in or modify some of the structure information and to use four dwDriverReservedN members of the DD_SYNCSURFACEDATA or three dwDriverReservedN members of the DD_SYNCVIDEOPORTDATA structure for its own purposes. These driver functions are required for the kernel-mode video transport support to work correctly.

A good example of how a driver can use these dwDriverReservedN members is to set a flag indicating which physical overlay an overlay surface is using if the hardware supports more than one physical overlay.
