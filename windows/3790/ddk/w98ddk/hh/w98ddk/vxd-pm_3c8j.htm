Implementing Power Management Policies

The following topics describe how to manage power to a device once the decision has been made to change its power state.

Implementing the policy can be as simple as a few lines of code. For example, in the OEM Service Release 2 (OSR2) release of Windows® 98, VCOMM is the power management policy owner for COM ports. When an application opens a COM port, VCOMM calls the configuration manager to turn on the COM port. When an application closes the COM port, VCOMM calls configuration manager to turn off the COM port.
