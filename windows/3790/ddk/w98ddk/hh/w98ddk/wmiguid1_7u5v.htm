WMI Considerations for Class/Port Drivers

Class drivers are drivers that receive IRPs and translate the IRPs into a private interface with miniport drivers. An example is the ScsiPort class driver that receives IRPs and translates the IRPs into SRBs.

In order to provide the extensibility functionality that is at the heart of WMI, a class driver needs to interrogate its miniport drivers for WMI data blocks that they want to support and make the IoWMIxxxx call on their behalf.
