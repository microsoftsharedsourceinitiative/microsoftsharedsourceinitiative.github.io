WMI Considerations for Drivers that Receive IRPs

Any driver that receives and processes IRPs can be a WMI driver. Microsoft will modify some, but not all, of the class/port drivers that ship with Windows® 98 and Windows® 2000 to provide WMI support. These class/port drivers receive IRPs and have a private interface to their miniport drivers. The private interface will be extended to support WMI.

A driver that receives IRPs needs to meet the following requirements in order to provide WMI functionality:

  • The driver needs to register its device object with WMI.
  • The driver needs to publish its schema to indicate the nature of the data the driver will provide.
  • The driver needs to handle WMI IRP_MJ_SYSTEM_CONTROL IRPs.
  • The driver needs to manage instance names for the data blocks it provides.

Notice that those device drivers that do not register with WMI to provide data still must forward the IRP_MJ_SYSTEM_CONTROL_IRPs to the next device in the device stack. The following topics describe these IRPs in detail.
