Video Drivers and the I2C Bus

Microsoft® Windows® 98 uses a Win32® driver model (WDM) streaming class driver to control video cards. Certain components on the video card (such as television tuners, video decoders, and video crossbars) are controlled by drivers that work with the class driver called minidrivers. Using minidrivers allows hardware vendors to create video drivers for various cards by simply installing the minidrivers that work with the devices on the card.

Several hardware manufacturers have adopted a modular approach to the building of video cards. This approach uses the I2C bus to connect devices to a master device. The master device sends commands to the devices for setting and reading the properties of the devices.

For video cards that use the I2C bus, the minidrivers tell the master device which device and what command to send on the I2C bus. The video class driver provided with Windows 98 provides an interface to minidrivers that handles the details of controlling the I2C bus.

To support Video Drivers and the I2C Bus, see the current DirectX DDK.
