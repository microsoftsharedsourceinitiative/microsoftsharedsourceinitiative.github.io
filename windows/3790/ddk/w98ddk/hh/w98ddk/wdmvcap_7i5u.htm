Stream class video capture minidrivers can support multiple, simultaneous streams of compressed and uncompressed video, timecode, closed caption, raw and decoded VBI data, and custom data formats. For each data type that can be produced simultaneously with other data types, the driver should create a new stream. Each stream is exposed as a separate kernel streaming pin by the stream class. These pins are mirrored by the DirectShow™ capture filter. For more information on pins and streaming, see Kernel Streaming.

Video streams are composed of time-stamped, digitized video and related information such as VBI and timecode. Streams can be paused, started, and stopped independently. Stream samples are time stamped with a 100nS resolution clock.

Each pin can support a variety of different formats. For example, a single pin could support RGB16, RGB24, YUV9, and JPEG digital video. It is expected that most devices will support only a few of these pins.

Streaming conventions are also applied to input pins on the capture filter that represent analog video flowing into the capture device, and to video port output connections for both images and VBI.
