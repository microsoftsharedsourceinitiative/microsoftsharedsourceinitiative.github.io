Introduction to Video Capture

Video capture minidrivers work in combination with the stream class driver to control hardware devices that produce streams of video images and related data. Video capture minidrivers provide the following functionality:

  • Capture of compressed and uncompressed video streams from digital and analog video sources such as 1394, USB, S-Video and RCA video-in jacks
  • Control of devices associated with video streams such as TV/radio tuner(s), signal routing devices (crossbars), TV audio control, and video compressors
  • Control of camera properties such as zoom, pan, and focus
  • Control of video properties such as hue, saturation, brightness and sharpness
  • Control of video ports and capture from video port streams
  • Capture of vertical blanking interval (VBI) data
  • Capture of timecode
  • Capture of ancillary data streams
  • WDM streaming (for kernel-mode) and DirectShow™ (for user-mode) compatibility
